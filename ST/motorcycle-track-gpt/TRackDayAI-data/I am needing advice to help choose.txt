The desired sag for road racing motorcycles typically involves setting both the static sag (the amount the bike compresses from its fully extended position under its own weight) and the rider sag (the amount the bike compresses from its fully extended position under the combined weight of the bike and the rider). These settings are crucial for optimal performance as they affect the motorcycle's handling characteristics, such as turn-in response, mid-corner stability, and traction during acceleration and braking.

Here are general guidelines for sag settings in road racing:

Front Sag (Rider Sag): Typically between 30mm to 35mm. This setting allows for enough travel to absorb track irregularities and maintain front tire contact for braking and cornering.

Rear Sag (Rider Sag): Usually set to around 25mm to 30mm. This is slightly less than the front to ensure that the rear of the bike is stable and provides good traction during acceleration out of corners.

Static Sag (Front and Rear): Generally, around 5mm to 10mm for both front and rear suspension. This ensures that the suspension is not too stiff, allowing it to react to smaller bumps and maintain tire contact with the track.

It's essential to measure and set the sag accurately, using a proper method and with the help of an assistant. Also, keep in mind that these are starting points and that fine-tuning will be required based on rider preference, track conditions, tire behavior, and the motorcycle's handling feedback.

For specific sag settings for your motorcycle model, you should consult the motorcycle's manual, a professional race setup technician, or look for guidelines provided by the suspension manufacturer. Racing forums and community groups can also be a good source of information as experienced racers often share their settings for different track conditions and motorcycles.
